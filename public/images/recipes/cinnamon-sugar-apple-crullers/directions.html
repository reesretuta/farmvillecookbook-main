Ingredients
  • 4 cups Wheat Flour
  • 1 package Dry Yeast
  • ¾ cup Milk
  • 7 tbs Granulated Sugar
  • 2 Eggs
  • 5 tbs Butter
  • A pinch of salt
  • ½ Lemon, zest and juice
  • Deep fat fryer with Oil (i.e. sunflower)
  • ½ lb Apples (Braeburn, Fiji, or Boskoop work best), cubed
  • 1 cup Sugar (for topping)
  • 1 tbs Cinnamon (for topping)
Directions
  1. Combine 1 cup of sugar and 1 tablespoon of cinnamon for the topping. Set aside.
  2. In a pot, bring milk and butter to a boil. Then add flour, yeast, sugar and salt.
  3. Stir together to create a smooth paste. Then remove from heat.
  4. Peel apples and cut them into 1-centimeter small cubes. Add the lemon juice and zest to eliminate browning.
  5. Using a mixer with a hook, mix apple pieces into the dough.
  6. Once apples are mixed in, add eggs one at a time.
  7. Let the dough rest for 20 - 30 minutes to allow the yeast to develop.
  8. Finally, the dough should be smooth. For a rustic look, form small dumplings with a tablespoon and fry them in hot oil. Do not crowd in the pot since the crullers will expand in the oil. For a more polished look, use a piping bag with a piping star nozzle tip (size 12), pipe dough into rings onto parchment paper. Place in the freezer for 20 minutes to make it easier to transfer to fry oil.
  9. Once fried, remove from oil and let cool for 30 seconds.
  10. Roll, toss and sprinkle the crullers in the sugar and cinnamon mixture. They are tastiest when served while hot.
