Ingredients
  • 1 lb Ripe Peaches
  • 4 tbs Unsalted Butter
  • ¼ cup Light Honey, such as Wildflower Honey
  • ¼ tsp Ground Cinnamon
  • ⅛ tsp Ground Nutmeg
  • 1 ¼ cups Granulated Sugar
  • 1 cup Heavy Cream
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 2 Eggs, room temperature
  • 1 ¾ cups Self-Rising Flour
  • ½ tsp Salt
Directions
  1. Preheat oven to 350° F.
  2. Drop peaches into a saucepan of boiling water for 15 - 30 seconds.
  3. Remove with a skimmer and rinse under cold running water. Rub off skins and slice the peaches about ¼ inch thick. You should have about 3 cups.
  4. Place a 10-inch cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add the butter, honey, cinnamon, nutmeg and ¼ cup of the sugar. Stir as the butter and sugar melt.
  5. Continue cooking, stirring constantly, until the syrup boils and thickens slightly, 2 - 3 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  6. Carefully arrange the peach slices in a decorative pattern, working from the outer edge to the center. Set aside.
  7. To make the batter, place the cream and vanilla in a mixing bowl and whip on medium-high speed until soft peaks form.
  8. Gradually beat in the remaining 1 cup of sugar in a steady stream and continue mixing until soft, do not over-mix.
  9. Add the eggs and beat until soft peaks form again.
  10. Sift the flour and salt over the top of the batter and fold in until evenly mixed.
  11. Gently pour the batter over the peaches in the skillet, spreading it evenly.
  12. Place the skillet in the oven with a baking sheet placed on the shelf below to catch any drips.
  13. Bake for about 40 minutes, until a toothpick or cake tester inserted in the center comes out clean.
  14. Let the cake stand for 5 - 10 minutes. Place a heatproof serving dish on top and carefully invert so that the cake drops straight down over the center of your dish.
  15. To serve, use a serrated knife to cut through the peaches and the cake. This is best served warm from the oven.
