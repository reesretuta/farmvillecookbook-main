Ingredients
Goat Cheese:
  • 1 quart Goat Milk, not ultra-pasteurized
  • ⅓ cup fresh Lemon Juice
Biscuits:
  • ¾ cup All-Purpose Flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ¼ cup Whole Wheat, All-Purpose or Pastry Flour
  • ⅛ tsp Baking Soda
  • ½ tbs Baking Powder
  • ½ tsp Salt
  • 3 tbs very cold, unsalted Butter, cubed
  • ½ cup Buttermilk
Blueberry Compote:
  • 1 ½ cup Blueberries
  • 3 tbs Water
  • 1 tbs fresh Lemon Juice
  • ¼ cup Sugar
  • 1 tsp Arrowroot Starch Flour or Cornstarch
  • 1 tsp Water
Directions
Goat Cheese:
  1. In a non-reactive pot, heat the goat milk slowly on the stove over low heat until it reaches about 180º F - 185º F. You should see gentle bubbles and the surface will start to look a bit foamy. Turn off and remove from heat.
  2. Stir in the lemon juice and let sit for 10 minutes. The milk should curdle and start to become a bit thicker on the surface.
  3. Line a colander with two layers of cheesecloth and very gently pour the milk-lemon mixture into the lined colander. Gather the cheesecloth up around the curds and tie into a bundle, using kitchen twine or a rubber band.
  4. Set the colander over a large bowl, pot, or jar, so the liquid (the whey) can drip out. Let drain for at least 1 ½ hours or overnight.
  5. Scrape the goat cheese off the cheesecloth and into a small bowl or airtight container (if not ready to use right away) with a spoon.
Blueberry Compote:
  1. Combine 1 cup of blueberries with 3 tablespoons water, lemon juice, and sugar in a small saucepan.
  2. Cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes, and then mash mixture with a potato masher until fairly smooth.
  3. Add remaining ½ cup blueberries.
  4. In a small bowl, combine arrowroot (or cornstarch) and 1 teaspoon of water and stir until completely smooth.
  5. Pour into blueberry mixture and raise the heat until compote reaches a gentle boil
  6. Boil for about 1 minute, or until lightly thickened. Mash again until desired texture is reached.
  7. Set aside.
Biscuits:
  1. Preheat oven to 450ºF.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine all dry biscuit ingredients. Then, using a pastry blender, two knives or a food processor, cut butter into the flour until it resembles course meal.
  3. Add buttermilk and mix until just combined. Over-stirring will result in tough biscuits.
  4. Turn out biscuit dough onto flour surface. Pat dough down until it's ½ inch thick, then fold dough about five times (using a dough scraper really helps!). Finally, pat down until about 1 inch thick.
  5. Cut into rounds using a round cookie cutter or mold. Place biscuits on a cookie sheet and bake for 10 – 12 minutes, or until biscuits are a light golden brown on top.
Assemble Biscuits:
  1. Slice warm biscuits in half and spread homemade goat cheese over the bottom half. Top with blueberry compote and serve.
